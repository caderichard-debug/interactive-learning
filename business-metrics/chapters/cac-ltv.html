CAC vs LTV and payback

CAC (customer acquisition cost) is what you spend to win a marginal customer. LTV (lifetime value) is the contribution you expect from that customer over their life. Decisions usually hinge on payback period and the margin of safety if churn is worse than modeled.

Definitions that actually match
  • Align CAC window with sales cycle length (blended S&M / new logos in the same period).
  • LTV should use contribution margin, not revenue, unless you explicitly mean “gross LTV.”
  • Payback = CAC ÷ monthly contribution; compare to your capital cost and strategic risk appetite.
Sensitivity

Small changes in retention or expansion NRR dominate long-horizon LTV. Run scenarios where expansion slows or churn inflects—those stress tests matter more than tweaking ad creative by 5%.
