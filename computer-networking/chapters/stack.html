Chapter · layering

Packet path across the stack

Each layer solves a different problem: link moves frames on a segment, network routes between hosts, transport multiplexes and repairs streams, application interprets bytes as protocol messages.

Why layers matter for debugging
  • Symptoms at the UI may originate in DNS, TLS cert chains, MTU black holes, or app-level head-of-line blocking.
  • Observability needs correlation IDs that survive hops—pure packet dumps rarely tell the product story alone.
Mental model

Think of a request as a stack of envelopes: each layer adds headers for its peer on the other host. Peeling envelopes from the wire upward is how parsers recover HTTP from TCP segments from IP packets from Ethernet frames.
