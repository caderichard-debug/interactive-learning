TCP handshake and retransmits

TCP establishes bidirectional state with SYN, SYN-ACK, ACK—three segments minimum before data (ignoring TFO). Loss on any segment triggers retransmission timers that interact with RTT estimates (RTO backoff).

Why you feel it

Microservices chatter and “one query per HTTP request” patterns multiply setup tax. Connection reuse, pooling, and TLS session resumption exist specifically to amortize this cost.

Retransmit intuition

The explorer’s packet stream slows with RTT and stutters with loss—mirroring the idea that goodput drops when ACKs are delayed or segments are missing. The explorer also shows a running retransmit event count and ties a toy cwnd to those drops.

TCP 3-way (minimum)

Before application data, the client and server exchange three segments to agree on sequence state: SYN, SYN-ACK, ACK. That is at least one round trip of control-plane work (more with options, middleboxes, or loss).
