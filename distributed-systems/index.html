Concept cards

Slot A

Partial failure dominates

A message may be slow, lost, or duplicated. You rarely observe “true” state across nodes—only timeouts and replies that must be interpreted under uncertainty.

Slot B

CAP is a lens, not a scorecard

Under partition, a system must choose how it behaves toward consistency vs availability for affected operations. Real products add nuance (latency, linearizability tiers, multi-dc rules).

Slot C

Consensus buys agreement, not speed

Protocols like Raft elect a leader and replicate a log so committed decisions survive faults—at the cost of round-trips and careful failure handling. Continue: Consensus sketch.
