Partition, quorum, leader term, and log append

Three sketches stack intentionally: topology vs policy (partition severity × quorum rule), then who may lead (terms and ballots—no network geometry), then how the log advances on a single leader view. All are toys—use chapters for vocabulary and failure modes.

1. Partition cut vs quorum threshold

Two different knobs: severity moves the cut and changes how many replicas sit on the majority side versus the isolated slice (topology). The quorum threshold slider does not move the cut—it only changes the fixed rule votes needed = ceil(N × threshold) computed on full N. The bar under the ring compares reachable count to votes needed for strict quorum writes.

2. Leader term (election rounds, schematic)

This panel is not a partition diagram: it only shows monotonic terms, candidate vs follower, and a split vote beat before one replica wins a majority of grants. No log entries—just the “who may propose” gate. Compare to sketch 1, where geometry blocks votes in a different way.

3. Log append + majority commit (schematic)

Assumes a stable leader term. The leader extends the log with index 4 after prefix 1–3. Entries in amber are not yet on a majority; green marks the toy “committed” view after three of four replicas hold the new slot (majority of four).

Reading the three sketches
  • Partition ≠ quorum: severity changes the reachable set; the threshold slider changes the rule on N. A bad combination blocks strict quorum work even when “many” nodes are alive on the minority side.
  • Term panel: same quorum math, but the story is ballots and liveness (split votes, backoff)—not message routing.
  • Log panel: append/catch-up and majority commit; omits RPC details, disk sync, and conflicting tails—see Log replication. Pair with Leader election.
