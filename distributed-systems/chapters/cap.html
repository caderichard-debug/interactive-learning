CAP & trade-offs

The CAP framing asks what your system does when a network partition prevents some nodes from talking. You cannot guarantee strong linear consistency for all operations and full availability for every request pattern simultaneously in the classic model—something has to give for the affected scope.

Three letters (compressed)
Letter Typical meaning in teaching Reality check
C All nodes agree on what a read returns relative to writes (strong forms = linearizability). Products expose tiers: session, causal, “read your writes,” etc.
A Every request eventually receives a non-error response (liveness for that definition). Degraded modes, shedding load, or partial outages reinterpret “available.”
P The system keeps progressing when messages are lost/delayed arbitrarily between groups. Partitions happen; “P” is less a knob than an admission of the real world.

Intuition: during a split, if two sides both accept conflicting writes without coordination, you risk divergent histories. If one side refuses writes to stay consistent, you sacrificed availability for those operations. Most production systems choose nuanced policies per data type and per region.
