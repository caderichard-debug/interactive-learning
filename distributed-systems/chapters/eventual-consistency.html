Eventual consistency

If replicas accept writes while disconnected, histories diverge. Eventual consistency means that if updates stop and enough connectivity exists, replicas tend toward the same state—often via anti-entropy, merge rules, or CRDT-shaped data structures.

What readers might see

Stale reads are common before convergence. Products advertise guarantees like “read your writes” or monotonic reads to narrow the surprise window. Conflict resolution (last-write-wins, application merge, CRDTs) is part of the data model, not an afterthought.

Contrast with strong consistency expectations under partition—different problem, different ops cost.
