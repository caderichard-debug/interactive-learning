Log replication

Consensus-shaped systems treat the source of truth as an append-only log: each slot has an index and payload. The leader appends; followers store copies; an entry becomes committed once a quorum has persisted it—so a single surviving majority can recover the same prefix after a crash or partition heals.

Vocabulary (compressed)
Term Typical meaning Why it matters
Log index Position in the replicated sequence (1-based in papers, 0-based in code—watch for off-by-one). Ordering claims reference indices, not wall clocks.
Match / next index Per-follower pointers describing how much of the leader’s log is known to agree. Drives incremental catch-up and repair after lag or leader change.
Commit (advance) Leader learns that ≥ majority have appended an entry; commit index moves forward. Application-visible “durability” is gated on commit rules + fsync policies.
Majority is the hinge

Two successive quorums overlap in at least one node. That overlap is why committed prefixes cannot silently fork in the idealized model—any new leader’s election path intersects someone who saw the prior commit point. Pair with Leader election for the “who may propose” half of the story.

After a leader change

A new leader may need to overwrite uncommitted suffixes on followers so every log matches a single prefix before new appends—Raft’s AppendEntries consistency check. What clients observe as “committed” still follows the same majority rule; the explorer animation only shows a happy path with no conflicting tails.
