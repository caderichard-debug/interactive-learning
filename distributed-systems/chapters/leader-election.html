Chapter · leadership

Leader election

A leader is a role, not magic: one replica is designated to propose ordering for a window of time so clients have a clear place to send work. Election picks that replica using ballots or terms; safety requires that at most one leader’s proposals can commit per term—otherwise you are back to split brain.

What an election is trying to fix
Idea Teaching role Reality check
Term / epoch / ballot number Monotonic clock for “which era of configuration are we in?” Stale leaders lose to higher terms. Must be persisted before claiming leadership; ties need deterministic rules.
Quorum of votes A candidate must hear from a majority before becoming leader—intersection with prior committed state. Same majority math as partition sketches; minority partitions cannot elect safely for strict models.
Split votes No candidate reaches a majority in one round; protocols randomize backoff and retry. Liveness tuning matters; storms of elections can hurt tail latency.
Election alone is not enough

Winning an election grants permission to try to commit new state, not a blank check. Durable log replication and fencing (see Partitions) keep storage and followers aligned when messages reorder or duplicate.

Raft-shaped vocabulary (names only)

Candidates bump a persisted term, send RequestVote RPCs, and become leader only after a majority of grants. Followers reject stale terms. That majority is evaluated against the full cluster size—so a candidate on the minority side of a partition cannot collect enough votes for safe leadership in the strict model, even if local processes keep running.
