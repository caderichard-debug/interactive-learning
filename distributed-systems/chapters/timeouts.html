Timeouts & retries

A slow response is indistinguishable from failure without extra channels (heartbeats, probes, out-of-band signals). Timeouts turn ambiguity into a decision: give up, retry, or fail over—each choice has blast radius.

Retry pressure
  • Retry storm: many clients retry together and overload a recovering dependency.
  • Duplication: the server may have executed the work but the client timed out—retries must be idempotent or deduplicated.
  • Jittered backoff: spreads retries in time; still tune max attempts and budgets.

Pair with Partitions—timeouts often first signal a partition or overload, not a simple bug.
