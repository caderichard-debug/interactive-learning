Partitions

A partition cuts communication between subsets of nodes. Each side may still run and accept work. Without rules, you risk split brain—two leaders, conflicting writes, and merges that are expensive or impossible to automate safely.

Mitigations (names, not recipes)
Idea What it buys Cost / caveat
Quorum writes/reads Majority intersection guarantees you talk to someone who saw recent state. Latency; minority side may be unavailable for strict quorums.
Leader + lease Serializes decisions for a window of time. Clock skew and lease length trade off safety vs liveness.
Fencing token Stale leader’s writes lose to storage that remembers the higher epoch. Storage must enforce; not all layers participate.

Quorum vs partition: the reachable set after a cut is a topology fact; quorum size is a policy on N. Compare the two sliders in sketch 1 on the Animated explorer before Consensus sketch.
