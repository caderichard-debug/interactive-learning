Primary visualization

Sampling: wins, trials, and the Wilson band

Drag sliders to change the observed number of wins and total trades. The cyan band is an approximate 95% Wilson score interval for the underlying hit rate. Schematic: binary outcomes, stationary i.i.d. intuition only—real strategies are neither binary nor i.i.d.

Why Wilson? For proportions, the Wilson interval behaves better than a naive normal approximation near 0 or 1 and with small n. It is still a teaching layer on top of a toy model—pair with Returns & series for continuous-return intuition.

  • Edge: push wins toward 0 or n with small n—watch the band swallow “certainty.”
  • Boundary: at wins = 0 or wins = n, intervals are one-sided in interpretation; still use costs and stationarity checks in real work.
