Risk & sizing

Edge without sizing is undefined—the same signal implies different wealth paths at different leverage. This chapter is intuition only: Kelly criteria, fractional sizing, drawdown psychology, and why single-number VaR is never enough.

Kelly (cartoon)

In a simplified binary bet with known win probability p and payoff odds, full Kelly maximizes long-run growth rate but produces violent path variance. Practitioners use fractional Kelly (e.g. ¼–½) to model parameter error and path risk.

Intuition: if your p and payoff estimates come from the same noisy sample as your signal, “full Kelly” double-counts confidence. Cartoon: shrink size until ruin probability under stress scenarios is tolerable—not until a spreadsheet says “optimal.”

Drawdowns vs averages

A positive mean return with high variance can still spend years underwater (max drawdown). Investors experience path, not expectation. Typical in practice: cap leverage, diversify hypotheses not just names, and separate risk budget (how much heat you allow) from signal strength.

VaR: one brick, not a house

Value-at-Risk answers “how bad is the qth percentile loss over a horizon?” It is silent on what happens beyond q (tail dependence, liquidity gaps, gap risk). Pair any VaR number with stress tests and liquidity analysis—see Costs & capacity.

Risk as process

  • Pre-trade — limits, fat-finger checks, max position per name/sector.
  • Live — real-time exposure vs limit; circuit breakers when model health degrades.
  • Post-trade — attribution (signal vs costs vs drift), log incidents, revise assumptions.
