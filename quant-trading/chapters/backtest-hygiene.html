Backtest hygiene

A clean backtest separates research mistakes from genuine uncertainty. Three recurring failure modes: leakage, multiple testing, and non-stationarity. A fourth—bad labels—often dominates in ML pipelines.

Checklist (intuition)
  • Leakage — future information in features or labels (e.g. random k-fold without embargo around events). Typical fix pattern: purged cross-validation with embargo (López de Prado)—still not a proof of live edge.
  • Snooping — many strategies tried on the same slice inflate winners. Intuition: hold out data you never tune on; report multiplicity; simulate under a null of no edge where feasible.
  • Regimes — parameters fit to one crisis may fail in the next liquidity regime. Walk-forward is a schematic for adaptation, not a guarantee.
  • Labels — triple-barrier (profit-take, stop-loss, vertical barrier) defines outcome horizons; mis-specified barriers bake fake alpha into supervised models.

When to pause publication

Signal What it might mean
In-sample ≫ out-of-sample Overfit, leakage, or regime mismatch—before claiming “robust.”
Performance jumps when you shuffle folds Split protocol may be leaking overlapping bars or corporate actions.
Turnover × spread > gross edge Economically zero after costs—revisit Costs.
