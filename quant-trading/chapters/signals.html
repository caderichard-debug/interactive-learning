Signals & alpha decay

A signal is a rule mapping data to positions (or weights). Alpha decay describes how predictive power weakens as others trade the same idea, as capacity fills, or as microstructure shifts. Implementation shortfall is what you hoped to earn minus what you keep after costs and delays.

Concept spine
  • Orthogonality — crowded factors move together; diversifying across tickers in the same factor bet is not diversifying hypotheses.
  • Half-life — Cartoon: treat edge like exponential decay for planning conversations, not physics.
  • Signal-to-noise — in liquid markets, many apparent patterns are economically small vs friction; combine with sampling and costs.

Factor crowding (qualitative)

When many funds run similar “value” or “momentum” definitions, coordinated unwinds can deepen drawdowns beyond what a Gaussian covariance matrix suggests. Intuition: monitor who else is paid on the same anomaly, not only your in-sample Sharpe.

Research hygiene tie-in

If you mine hundreds of signals, winners will correlate with data snooping bias. Pre-register primary hypotheses where possible; disclose search space size; use purged CV / embargo splits when labels overlap in time.

Out of scope here

Alternative data licensing, order-book alpha, and execution algorithms—defer to specialist texts and compliance review. For capital preservation framing, continue to Risk & sizing.
