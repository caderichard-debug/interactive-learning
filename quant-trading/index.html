Concept cards

Slot A

Signals live in a noisy channel

A strategy’s observed win rate mixes true edge (if any), regime, and sampling luck. Intuition: tighten claims only when intervals shrink—see the explorer.

Slot B

Backtests estimate the past, not the future

Walk-forward design and purged cross-validation (see López de Prado) reduce leakage and overfitting—they do not guarantee out-of-sample performance.

Slot C

Execution and capacity eat “paper” returns

Slippage, fees, borrow, and market impact move realized P&L. Typical in practice: model turnover and liquidity before scaling size.

Slot A

Returns are messy time series

Volatility clusters; tails are fat; “i.i.d. Gaussian” is a cartoon for intuition, not a license for naive σ-scaling. Continue: Returns & series.

Sources (starting points)

  • López de Prado, Advances in Financial Machine Learning (Wiley, 2018) — labeling, leakage, purged CV.
  • Campbell, Lo, MacKinlay, The Econometrics of Financial Markets (Princeton, 1997) — returns, predictability, stylized facts.
  • Cont, “Empirical properties of asset returns: stylized facts and statistical issues” (2001) — qualitative feature set for time series.
  • Grinold & Kahn, Active Portfolio Management (McGraw-Hill, 1999) — information ratio and implementation shortfall framing.
  • Aronson, Evidence-Based Technical Analysis (Wiley, 2006) — data snooping and statistical rigor in trading research.
